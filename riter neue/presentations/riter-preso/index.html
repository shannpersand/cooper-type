Timeline

A pretty short timeline of the life and death of Riter Neue.
The very first digitized characters of Riter Neue. They followed Trajan Capital proportions and attempted to take cues from Carolingian miniscules.
Some initial serif explorations trying to capture the calligraphic movement of Carolingian miniscules.
Midway through the first term, I felt that the characters were falling over backwards a bit and needed to have some supporting weight on the other side.
I carried on developing this style of serif and ended up with some very round character shapes. I finished out the character set for the first term, though not particularly happy with the results.
When picking up the italic for the second semester, I ended up needing to revisit the roman. I was never really happy with the serifs or the shapes and now that I was starting to build out the italic I saw more things about the roman I wanted to change.
The italic came relatively quickly, well the lowercase at least. I was somewhat happy with the italic, definitely more excited about it than the roman. It seemed to fit togther better as a whole.
I continually revisited my roman trying to improve it and went through many many serif iterations. I also changed the weights of characters and the shapes. There was a lot of hemming and hawing about serif shapes as I tried to simplify the actual points to avoid additional headaches.
Then, with a three or so weeks remaining in the second term, I said “fuck it” and started over!
